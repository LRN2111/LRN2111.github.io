Chicken Satay Salad

Servings: 4

Total time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp medium curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 2 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 tsp clear honey
  • 600g skinless chicken breast fillets
  • 2 tbsp crunchy peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp sweet chilli sauce
  • 2 tbsp lime juice (approximately 1 lime)
  • Rapeseed oil
  • 4 Little Gem lettuces
  • ½ cucumber, sliced
  • Handful of baby plum tomatoes, quartered
  • Generous handful coriander, chopped
  • 50g peanuts or cashews, roughly chopped

Method

  1. Marinade for the chicken: Combine the soy sauce, curry powder, cumin, garlic and honey. Slice the chicken breasts in half lengthways, then add to the marinade and mix well. Set aside in the fridge for at least 1 hour, or overnight.
  2. Make the dressing: Mix the peanut butter, chilli sauce, lime juice and 2 tbsp water to make a spoonable sauce.
  3. When ready to cook the chicken, wipe a large lidden frying pan with a little oil. Fry the chicken on a medium heat, covered with the lid, for 5 minutes. Turn the chicken over and cook for a further 2 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked but still moist. Rest for a few minutes.
  4. While the chicken cooks and rests, prepare the rest of the salad and pile on to plates. Slice the chicken, add this to the plates and spoon over the sauce. Serve sprinkled with the nuts.
