Spinach and Feta Lasagne

Servings: 6

Total time: 2 hours

Ingredients

  • 3 small onions, chopped
  • 6 ribs of celery, sliced
  • 450g mushrooms, roughly diced
  • 6 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 3 tsp mixed Italian herbs
  • 600g passata
  • 300g spinach leaves
  • 1 tbsp sugar (approximately), to taste
  • 50g butter
  • 50g plain flour
  • 500ml milk
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Freshly grated nutmeg
  • 30g grated parmesan
  • 100g grated cheddar
  • 15 lasagne sheets (approximately)
  • 150g feta cheese

Method

  1. In a large pan, fry the onion and celery in a little oil on a low heat.
  2. Once the onion is starting to soften, add the garlic and mushrooms and fry for a few minutes.
  3. Add the passata, mixed herbs and season with salt and pepper. Stir and simmer for about 20 minutes, until the sauce is reduced.
  4. Taste and add sugar if needed, then tear in the spinach leaves. Simmer until the spinach is wilted and you have a thick sauce.
  5. Meanwhile, make the cheese sauce. Melt the butter in a small saucepan, and once melted, add the flour. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly with a whisk.
  6. Add the bay leaf, then gradually add the milk, whisking well with each addition.
  7. Once all the milk has been added, grate in some nutmeg and parmesan, and season with pepper. Keep whisking until the sauce thickens, then stir in half the cheddar.
  8. Preheat the oven to 170Â°C (fan), and lightly oil a large ovenproof dish.
  9. Assemble the lasagne. Spread a thin layer of the spinach mixture into the dish, then crumble over some feta. Add a single layer of lasagne sheets (being careful not to overlap them), then spread over a few spoonfuls of cheese sauce. Repeat this until the spinach mixture is used up and you are reaching the top of the dish.
  10. Scatter the remaining cheddar over the final cheese sauce layer, then bake for 40-45 minutes.
