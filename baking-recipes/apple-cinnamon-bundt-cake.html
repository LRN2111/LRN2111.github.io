Apple and Cinnamon Bundt Cake

Servings: 12

Total time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

For the cake:

  • 300ml vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 200g light brown soft sugar
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 375g plain flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1¼ tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 2 large apples, chopped
  • 100g walnuts, chopped (plus extra to decorate)

For the icing:

  • 80g icing sugar
  • 2 tsp butter, softened
  • 2 tbsp salted caramel sauce
  • 1-2 tbsp water

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 150 °C. Generously butter a large Bundt cake tin (ideally around 2.5 litres), covering all the nooks and crannies. Dust with flour.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the oil, sugars and vanilla extract, then add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  3. Combine the flour, salt, baking powder and spices in a separate bowl, then add to the liquid mixture.
  4. Fold in the chopped apples and nuts.
  5. Spoon the mixture into the prepared Bundt tin and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour 10 minutes to 1 hour 20 minutes, or until an inserted skewer comes out clean.
  7. Cool on a rack in the tin for 10 minutes, then turn out onto the rack.
  8. Once cool, make the icing. Combine the icing sugar with the butter and caramel sauce, then beat in the water a little at a time until the icing is the desired consistency.
  9. Spoon the icing over the cooled cake, then decorate with more chopped nuts.
